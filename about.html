About

Someone finally reads the water

If you hold a professional license, you're standing in a current — rules shifting around you all the time, mostly without notice, never with explanation. Regulators publish changes into dockets nobody reads. The notices, when they arrive, say what the new text is and never what it means.

Compliance Currents exists to close that gap. We monitor the primary sources directly — state rulemaking dockets, board rules, federal registers — and when something changes that touches your license or your agency, you get a short brief from someone who actually read the rule: what changed, when it took effect, and why it matters for how you practice.

Every claim is cited. Every date is verified. And nothing runs more than thirty days past its effective date, because a late brief is a worthless one.

How we work

Detection is automated: our monitoring system watches the rulemaking record continuously, so changes get flagged the moment they enter the public docket. Judgment never is: every flagged change is verified against the primary source and translated by a person before anything reaches you. Flagged by the system, verified against the record — that order, every time.

Who writes this
