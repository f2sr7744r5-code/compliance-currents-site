How every brief works

Three questions, always in the same order

Regulators publish the record. We translate it — in a short brief you can read in minutes, with every claim tied to a primary source.

What changed

The specific rule, cited to the primary source. We prove the difference between the old text and the new — never just assert that something changed.

When it changed

The effective date, front and center. A brief that arrives late is worthless, so every brief prints its own dates: when the rule took effect and when we delivered.

Why it matters

Translated for your license type or agency setting. This is the part the notices always skip — and the reason we exist.

Who we cover

Behavioral health — Colorado

  • Medicaid, accreditation, and federal confidentiality standards
